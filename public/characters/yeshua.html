Growing Up with Yeshua

Character Introduction

He Sees What Others Walk Past

Yeshua sees the world differently.

While others move quickly, he observes, thinks, and creates. He notices the way light falls through a window, the pattern in a cracked sidewalk, the story hidden inside an ordinary afternoon. To Yeshua, everything is material — everything has the potential to become something more.

Sometimes that makes him feel out of place. Like he doesn't quite fit the speed of the room, the rhythm of the group, the easy confidence of the kids who seem to know exactly where they belong.

But through building, drawing, and imagining, Yeshua begins to understand something that will change everything: being different is not a weakness. It's his purpose.

What Yeshua Carries

🌊

Feeling Different

In a world that moves fast and loud, Yeshua moves slow and quiet. He often wonders if there's something wrong with him — why he doesn't feel the things others seem to feel so easily, why he sees so much when others see so little.

🌀

Overthinking

His mind never stops. He replays conversations, re-draws plans, imagines seventeen versions of the same outcome. The gift that makes him creative is the same thing that sometimes keeps him up at night, turning an ordinary moment into a labyrinth.

🕯️

Quiet Confidence

Yeshua rarely speaks first. He watches, he considers, and when he does speak, what he says tends to matter — but getting to that place, trusting that his voice is worth something, is a journey he is still walking.

✏️

The Creative Gift

He builds things. He draws them first, then builds them. In the space between the sketch and the finished object, Yeshua finds a kind of peace he cannot find anywhere else — a certainty that he belongs, that his hands are doing exactly what they were made to do.

His Journey

What Yeshua Learns to Carry Differently

01

Patience Through Creation

Yeshua discovers that the best things — the things worth making — cannot be rushed. Every project he works on teaches him that patience is not passive. It is active, intentional, and deeply creative. The waiting is part of the building.

02

Confidence in Who He Is

Slowly, through the small victories of finished things — a drawing that captures what he meant, a structure that stands — Yeshua begins to trust himself. Not because anyone told him to. Because he proved it to himself, quietly, in the space where he creates.

03

His Mind Is a Gift

The thing he struggled with most — the mind that never stops, the way he sees everything — turns out to be the most valuable thing he has. He learns to stop apologising for how he thinks and start using it. His perspective is not a problem. It is a power.

🏠

Family Dynamic

Navigating Two Homes

Yeshua's story is set against the reality of a divorce household — a landscape that many children live in and few stories honestly inhabit. He moves between two homes, two rhythms, two versions of everyday life. Some days the transition is smooth. Other days, it costs something.

What Yeshua is learning — slowly, imperfectly — is that stability is not about place. A child can feel grounded when their world has two addresses. The ground is not beneath his feet. It is inside him. And that discovery is one of the most important things his story teaches.
